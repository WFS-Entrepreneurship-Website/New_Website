our history

Woodberry Forest School Entrepreneurship Club (WFSEC) is dedicated to the discovery of potential, development of leadership, and promotion of innovation. The club's principal constituencies are students, staff, faculty, and alumni of WFS. Since its establishment in early 2020 by founder Ben Zhou and co-founder Reed Graff, WFSEC has served as a bridge between the Woodberry Alumni Association and current members of the school.

2020

  • WFSEC was founded with 20 members under Ben Zhou and Reed Graff.

2021

  • Club reaches 80 members with multiple guest speaker events and education seminars.

2022

  • The first Paragon Challenge and Job Convention Hosted at State-level

2023

  • Nationwide Expansion

Our Mission

Through the toils of early life many lose sight of the future ahead. Our mission is to educate students and members on entrepreneurial endeavors. We focus on teaching members how to manage a business, start a business from the ground up, how to work with a team, and ultimately how to be successful through hands-on practices.

What Notable Events Do We Offer?

Throughout the year we offer many keynote events, here are just a few highlights that makes us unique:

Educational Seminars

We offer monthly conferences with established entrepreneurs, and notable school alumni. This comes in the form of lectures, workshops, and other interactive activities voted upon by the members.

learn more

Paragon Challenge

An annual challenge that offers prizes to students from across the US to compete in entrepreneurial related activities for connections, investors, scholar recognition, and cash prizes.

learn more

Annual Internship Convention

On the final judging day of our Paragon Challenge, we host a job convention in which students and alumni get together and coordinate summer internships, shadowing opportunities, and mentorships.
